“Machine behaviour in the age of large language models: social, cognitive and evolutionary perspectives”
 
Interface Focus publishes theme issues on emerging topics at the boundary of the life, physical and social sciences. Formed of high‑quality articles, they aim to facilitate cross‑disciplinary research across these traditional divides by acting as a forum that is accessible to all. The journal brings together original research, reviews and opinions on a topic to provide an overview of the field and a showcase of research in that area. More information can be found at: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rsfs. 
 
The topic has already been approved by the Editorial Board, and the issue will definitely go ahead. The issue will use large language models (LLMs) as a concrete test case for the emerging science of machine behaviour. It aims to (i) empirically characterise how LLMs and related AI systems act, interact and shape their environments; (ii) develop social, cognitive and evolutionary models of AI behaviour, drawing on concepts such as trust, cooperation, reciprocity, cultural evolution and theory of mind; and (iii) understand how LLMs reshape human groups, institutions and norms, and how we might align machine behaviour with human values and social goals. We feel that this topic is especially well suited to the broad, cross‑disciplinary scope of Interface Focus. 
 
Given your research interests and recent works in this area, I am writing to ask whether you would be willing to contribute a paper to this issue. This could take the form of an original research article, a methodological paper, or a perspective/review that synthesises existing work and outlines key open questions for the field. If you are interested in contributing, please send me (within two weeks if possible) 
 
- A tentative title (this should be short and of broad interest)
- The name and affiliation of the lead author and the names of any co-authors if known at this stage
- The paper type (original research, review, opinion piece etc)
- A brief description of the information to be presented in the article.
 
 
The paper should be approximately 6,000 words. We would be looking to receive papers by 30/09/2026. All papers will be fully peer reviewed and revised as required. More information for authors can be found here: https://royalsocietypublishing.org/rsfs/pages/for-authors.
